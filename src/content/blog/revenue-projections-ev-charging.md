# What Does an EV Charger Actually Earn? Revenue Projections Explained

When property owners ask about EV charging revenue, they usually expect a vague answer. The truth is more useful: there are real projection ranges, grounded in actual utilization data, and the key variable is straightforward enough to reason about.

This article explains the numbers, what drives them, and how to think about revenue realistically for your property type.

## The Projection Framework

Evium's NEVI proposal data establishes three utilization scenarios for a 180kW DC fast charger:

- **Low utilization:** approximately $900/month — $54,000 over five years
- **Medium utilization:** approximately $1,500/month — $90,000 over five years
- **High utilization:** approximately $2,100/month — $126,000 over five years

These figures represent gross charging revenue per unit. In a no-cost model where Evium covers all capital and operational costs, the property owner receives a percentage of this — typically around 10% — as passive income. In an owner-investment model, the property keeps 100% of revenue.

The spread between low and high is meaningful: more than 2x over five years, on the same hardware, at the same location. That's why utilization is the critical conversation.

## What Drives Utilization

Utilization is a function of how often the charger is in use and for how long. Several factors determine this:

**Traffic volume.** A DC fast charger on a highway corridor with high daily vehicle throughput will outperform one at a low-traffic retail location. This is why NEVI sites are placed at fuel/rest locations along major corridors — the traffic pattern is known and high.

**Dwell time.** Charging time aligns with how long a driver is on-site. DC fast chargers are designed for high-turnover locations where drivers stop for 20–45 minutes. Level 2 chargers match longer dwell times — workdays, overnight stays, medical visits.

**EV density in the user base.** A multifamily property with a high percentage of EV-owning tenants will have consistent overnight Level 2 utilization. A highway rest stop serves a cross-section of the driving population, which grows with EV adoption.

**Pricing and payment friction.** Session pricing and payment experience affect whether drivers use a charger or choose another. Evium's platform supports multiple payment methods and integrates with the driver app, reducing friction.

**Network visibility.** Being discoverable in navigation apps and EV routing software increases utilization. Connected chargers on managed networks — including Evium's — are indexed in these systems.

## Level 2 vs. DC Fast: Different Revenue Profiles

The NEVI projections above are for DC fast charging, which is a different product than Level 2.

DC fast chargers generate revenue through shorter, higher-cost sessions at high-traffic locations. The revenue ceiling per charger is higher, but so is the cost of the equipment and installation.

Level 2 charging in multifamily or commercial settings generates revenue through longer, lower-cost sessions at lower-traffic locations. Revenue per session is smaller, but utilization can be very consistent — particularly with overnight residential charging — and the total cost basis is much lower.

For most multifamily and commercial deployments, Level 2 is the right product. For highway corridors, retail destinations, and high-turnover parking, DC fast is appropriate. Some properties benefit from a mix.

## Honest Caveats

Revenue projections are projections. Actual performance depends on the specific location, EV adoption in the user base, pricing decisions, and operational uptime.

A few things worth understanding:

Utilization builds over time. New installations typically see lower utilization in early months as tenants or visitors discover the chargers and integrate them into their routines. Revenue tends to grow as EV ownership in the user base grows.

Uptime matters. A charger that's offline doesn't generate revenue. Evium's 24/7 remote monitoring and maintenance is directly tied to revenue optimization — resolving outages quickly keeps utilization rates up.

Pricing affects behavior. Session pricing that's too high reduces utilization. Pricing that's too low leaves revenue on the table. Evium's platform supports dynamic pricing and configuration changes through the owner portal without requiring physical access to the unit.
