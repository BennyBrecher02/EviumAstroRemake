# Evium Business Model

## Four Financial Models

### 1. No-Cost Model (Primary / Most Common)
- EVIUM covers 100% of capital and operational costs
- EVIUM handles all management, maintenance, rebates
- Revenue split: typically ~90% EVIUM / ~10% property owner
- Property gains charging amenity + passive income stream with zero investment
- **This is Evium's primary selling point and most profitable model for them**

### 2. Client Investment Model
- Property owner finances and owns the full solution
- Property manages and maintains (or pays for service)
- Property receives 100% of charging revenue
- EVIUM assists with rebate processing only

### 3. Hybrid Model
- Blend of no-cost and investment models
- Custom balance of shared investment and shared revenue
- Ideal for clients wanting partial involvement

### 4. Leasing Solution
- Customizable monthly/annual leasing terms
- Property uses equipment without owning it
- Alternative to full ownership or EVIUM-investment

## Revenue Projections (per NEVI Proposal — Single 180kW DC Fast Charger)
- Low utilization: ~$900/month → $54,000 over 5 years
- Medium utilization: ~$1,500/month → $90,000 over 5 years
- High utilization: ~$2,100/month → $126,000 over 5 years

## Special Programs

### NEVI Program (National EV Infrastructure)
- Federal grant-backed infrastructure program
- No-cost to site owners — EVIUM as CPO
- Includes permitting, installation, management, compliance
- 5-year contract with 5-year renewal option
- Site host receives % of charging revenue

### Landlord "Free Commercial Charging" Program
- Zero-capital offering specifically for landlords
- Positions EV charging as tenant amenity
- Revenue share with landlord
- Evium handles all complexity

### Referral Program
- Incentivizes existing clients to refer other property owners
- Commission/revenue-share structure for referrals

## Customer Journey (5 Stages)
1. Initial Assessment — site address list, feasibility evaluation
2. Preparation & Planning — site walk, proposal development
3. Agreement & Compliance — contract, rebate applications, permits
4. Implementation — installation, configuration, commissioning
5. Ongoing Support — maintenance, monitoring, revenue optimization
